# Lidhra visual identity

## Core idea
The mark represents two independent paths that meet, exchange, and continue. It is derived from the Albanian root **lidh**, “to connect” or “to bind”. It must never be presented as a chain-link clip-art symbol, torrent glyph, magnet, or infinity sign.

## Preferred logo
Use the gradient mark on light or dark neutral surfaces. Use the white monochrome mark on photography or saturated backgrounds. The dark monochrome mark is for one-colour printing and symbolic system icons.

## Clear space
Keep clear space equal to one quarter of the mark’s width on every side. Never place text or controls inside that area.

## Minimum sizes
- Full mark: 20 px digital, 6 mm print.
- Simplified monochrome mark: 16 px digital.
- Horizontal lockup: 180 px minimum width.

## Colour language
- Teal starts the connection.
- Green represents active movement.
- Lime is used only as the terminal highlight, never as a large background.
- Download and seeding colours remain semantically distinct from the brand gradient.
- Dark surfaces use Ink 950; light surfaces use Mist 50 or pure white.

## Product language
Use **transfer**, **connection**, **server**, **activity**, **queue**, and **remote**. Avoid piracy-coded language, jokes about copyrighted media, or visual references to magnets and pirate symbols.

## Platform expression
- Apple: translucent materials, restrained gradient, native typography, no custom chrome.
- Android: Material 3 dynamic colour may tint surfaces, but the mark keeps its canonical gradient.
- Windows: Mica/Acrylic surfaces and Fluent geometry.
- Linux: libadwaita spacing, symbolic icons, adaptive layouts.

## Do not
Do not rotate, outline, stretch, add external glows, recolour individual path segments, or place the gradient mark on a competing green gradient.
